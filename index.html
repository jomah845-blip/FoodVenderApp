ðŸ”’ Privacy Protection & Ethical Framework

Our platform prioritizes participant privacy and adheres to strict ethical guidelines for digital health surveillance.

ðŸ”

Data Anonymization

Consumer Protection:

  • All personal identifiers (names, emails, phone numbers, addresses) are removed before analysis
  • Social media data is aggregated at group level - no individual tracking
  • Geolocation data is rounded to ZIP code level (no precise GPS coordinates stored)
  • Demographic data is generalized to prevent re-identification
  • Health information follows HIPAA de-identification standards (Safe Harbor method)

Vendor Protection:

  • Outbreak alerts shared only with health departments, not public initially
  • Vendors notified privately before any public disclosure
  • Right to respond and appeal automated risk assessments
  • Business information protected during investigation period
ðŸ‘ï¸

Public Data Only

Data Collection Boundaries:

  • Public social media posts only - No access to private messages, DMs, or closed groups
  • Published reviews - Only publicly available Yelp/Google reviews, no private feedback
  • Official health records - Public inspection reports only (no individual medical records)
  • No surveillance - No tracking of individual users across platforms
  • No data purchase - No buying personal data from data brokers

Consent Model:

  • Vendors opt-in voluntarily to join platform and share safety data
  • Consumers informed via app privacy policy about data analysis
  • Clear opt-out mechanisms for both groups
âš–ï¸

Legal Compliance

Regulatory Adherence:

  • HIPAA Compliance: Health information de-identified per 45 CFR Â§164.514
  • GDPR (EU): Data minimization, purpose limitation, right to erasure
  • CCPA (California): Consumer data rights, disclosure requirements
  • FDA Guidance: Follows FDA digital health innovation action plan
  • IRB Approval: Research protocol approved by Institutional Review Board

Data Governance:

  • Data retention: Maximum 2 years, then permanently deleted
  • Encryption: AES-256 at rest, TLS 1.3 in transit
  • Access controls: Role-based, multi-factor authentication
  • Audit logs: All data access tracked and reviewed quarterly
ðŸ¤

Ethical AI & Fairness

Algorithm Transparency:

  • Machine learning models regularly audited for bias
  • No discrimination based on vendor size, cuisine type, or neighborhood
  • Human review required before flagging any vendor as high-risk
  • Explainable AI - vendors can see why they were flagged
  • Regular validation against health department confirmed cases

Preventing Harm:

  • False positive monitoring - track and minimize incorrect alerts
  • Vendor appeals process - independent review panel
  • No automated enforcement - alerts inform human decisions only
  • Cultural sensitivity in NLP models (multiple languages, food terms)
ðŸ“‹

Data Minimization

What We Collect:

  • Public health-related keywords only (symptoms, food safety terms)
  • Temporal patterns (date/time of posts)
  • General location (city/ZIP code level)
  • Vendor safety ratings (public health inspection scores)
  • Aggregate sentiment scores (not individual opinions)

What We DON'T Collect:

  • âŒ Names, photos, or personal identifiers

  • âŒ Precise GPS coordinates or home addresses
  • âŒ Medical histories or treatment records
  • âŒ Private communications or social connections

Security Measures

Technical Safeguards:

  • Encryption: All data encrypted using industry standards (AES-256)
  • Secure Storage: HIPAA-compliant cloud infrastructure (AWS/Azure HIPAA eligible)
  • Access Control: Zero-trust architecture, least privilege principle
  • Monitoring: 24/7 intrusion detection and prevention systems
  • Backups: Encrypted, geographically distributed, air-gapped

Organizational Safeguards:

  • Annual security awareness training for all staff
  • Background checks for personnel with data access
  • Incident response plan tested quarterly
  • Regular third-party security audits
  • Breach notification protocols (72-hour reporting)

Mapping disease hotspots by location, time of day, weather conditions, and event gatherings to identify high-risk scenarios.

Can machine learning models predict outbreak risk based on multi-source data integration?

Combining social media data, health inspection records, weather patterns, and customer reviews to create predictive risk models.
